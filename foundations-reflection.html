What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  • Being hard on oneself is counter-productive, even if there are challenges and obstacles and you are slipping behind or not getting things, be kind to oneself, not just to others.
  • That I need to overcome years of bad habits, such as dealing with learning blocks in a more productive manner than just throwing my head against a wall repeatedly.
  • Abandon ego and ask for help early on, help is not cheating so long as it isn't simply one person giving you the answer

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values help stablised one's responses in response to varying degrees of stress and build a character of a person that others will respect and love.

Empathy builds genuine connections with others and helps demonstrate to oneself how one should act towards oneself. It's often easiest to treat others better than you treat yourself.

Self-awareness, at least in principle, is part of the solution to finding a way to deal with all manner of negative or challenging experiences or self-talk. Self-awarness is the key to unlocking freedom from the confines of one's own past experiences and trauma.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

That it offered the opportunity for so much potential growth when at my stage of life and experience I thought that I had a decent grasp of self and the world.

It was both humbling and inspiring.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging aspect was detaching myself and reassessing anew my deep seated attitudes to myself and others, that vividly conflicts with how I present myself on the surface, and detecting and grasping with these conflicts from a values and identity perspective.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because ultimately the ethos of EDA is to on a very simplistic level to ensure that every one of the students is given the full opportunity to be in a position to make the world a slighlty better place, by giving the chance for each of us to be more self-aware, to have better people and communcations skills to and to be better people for ourselves, and to be far more effective programmers.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I think that the time spent studying core learning is very important.

It establishes a new baseline of awareness and core skills, and helps crystalise values and sets up students for bootcamp to ensure greater group connection, synergy and co-operation such that we will hopefully be far more able to learn, whilst supporting and bonding with each other thus maximising not only the opportunity for a successful bootcamp, on an individual level but also on a broader an organisational and community level as we join the wider Tech community.
