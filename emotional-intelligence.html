What is Emotional Intelligence?

At the heart of EQ is the ability to identify and manage ones own emotions as well as the emotions of others.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is measured by knowledge of subjects and information and the ability to complete maths tasks in a standardised testing model. IQ represents abilities such as:
  • Visual and spacial processing
  • Visual and spacial processing
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Working memory and short-term memory
On the otherhand EQ relates to abilities such as:
  • Identifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling one's own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication
  • Relating to others
EQ is also not measureable.

Why is it important?

The views of many is that success in the workplace (and indeed the home) is more dependent on EQ than IQ. In some articles writers/researchers state that success is 80% EQ and only 20% IQ. Further to success the view is that greater EQ will lead to greater happiness that IQ on its own will not achieve.
